Welcome to the main page!

Our chosen student regulation is 'Chapter 4 = Offenses & Categories'. We chose this category as we found that most people in Elyon Christian School don't follow most of these rules, and we want to encourage people in reading this and follow it.

Mild Offenses

  • -Disrupting class order, including during online learning.
  • -Being late to school or entering class late.
  • -Using school facilities not meant for learning purposes without permission.
  • -Using school facilities outside school hours without permission.
  • -Not maintaining cleanliness or wearing accessories that do not comply with school rules.
  • -Littering in school areas.
  • -Using mobile phones or communication devices without permission during lessons.
  • -Leaving class or school area without permission.
  • -Bringing or playing games or sports equipment without permission in school areas or events.

Moderate Offenses (or Repeated Mild Offenses)

  1. Engaging in any form of buying or selling transactions (goods or services) on school premises.
  2. Failing to wear the proper school uniform.
  3. Bringing or parking bicycles, motorcycles, or electric vehicles in school areas without permission.
  4. Causing damage to school facilities — the student must replace or repair the damage.
  5. Leaving school or school-related activities during active hours without proper permission.
  6. Misusing school facilities such as projector remotes, air conditioning controls, elevators, etc.
  7. Damaging, losing, or removing official school property.
  8. Spreading false or exaggerated rumors about promotion or graduation.
  9. Creating disturbance or noise in school.
  10. Taking another person’s belongings without permission in school.

Severe Offenses (or Repeated Moderate Offenses)

  • Engaging in bullying in any form — verbal, physical, written, or visual — that is threatening or harassing, especially if repeated.
  • Defaming or discrediting the school through any form or medium.
  • Engaging in fights inside or outside the school.
  • Engaging in any form of cheating during examinations.
  • Leaving school or activities without official permission from the school.
  • Using, possessing, or distributing cigarettes, vapes, alcohol, or other addictive substances inside or outside school.
  • Bringing, storing, or using narcotics or illegal drugs inside or outside school.
  • Threatening or harassing teachers, staff, or fellow students in any manner.
  • Taking someone else’s belongings without permission.
  • Being involved in youth delinquency groups or prohibited organizations.
  • Engaging in criminal acts, whether inside or outside school.
  • Falsifying or manipulating school documents or information.
  • Participating in or organizing school-related activities outside the school without permission.
  • Involving external parties to resolve internal school issues.
